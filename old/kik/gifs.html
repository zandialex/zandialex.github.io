GIFs on Kik

Making Sharing Easy

Context

After Kik acquired Relay, a GIF messaging app, we decided to add GIFs to the app. I was lucky enough to design the first GIF experience as a member of the content team.

Chat recents
Taking a photo in a chat

The Problem

"How can we improve the expressive ability of our users?"

We knew our users had a more intimate chat experience when they used our other Kik content (smileys, stickers). But there was still friction when trying to communicate emotions, or trying to share comments about pop culture or memes. We wanted to improve this experience and thought GIFs might be a good way.

Setting Goals & Constraints

Given our content providers and the design goal, we brainstormed ideas for what to include in the product. We also needed to think about ways to potentially make money from this in the future. We came up with ideas for what we could include, then eliminated options we didn't think were feasible. We also needed to make every screen work with our half + full screen modes.

Discovery and Relevance

We explored various ways to let people discover GIFs. We wanted something that could always be relevant, simple, and easy to use. We ended up using live tiles that gave us the flexibility to update whatever new memes were relevant at the time or to reflect happenings in pop culture.

Search

It’s hard to quickly find a relevant GIF. We struggled with this one because we wanted to have a rich conversation experience, without too much friction around finding a relevant GIF. We did lots of explorations.

Sharing

We thought deeply about whether we should send a GIF right after a tap to speed up sharing. These discussions were among me, my team, and the head of product. We decided to show a preview of the GIF to align with our goal of keeping Kik a safe place (not all GIFs might be family-friendly), and it would be consistent with our photo sharing experience.

I also worked on the bubbles and icon (we had a UI pattern to always show the type of content and an icon), this was later abandoned though in favour of a simpler experience.

The colors represented the emotional nature of a GIF, and the pixelation was for its raw user-generated nature.

Looking Back

Since this was my first internship, there are lots of things I would have done better. My process was not very refined, so we often went back in circles. Sometimes I would go high fidelity too early on. However, this was a great experience because I learned lots about product development and the design process in general.

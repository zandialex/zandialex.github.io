Texting is keeping us apart

Most communication is carried out non-verbally, yet the most common form of digital communication is text. Much information is lost through text that's present when face-to-face.

Unfortunately, chatting online feels like passing letters under a closed door. Friends feel separated behind a barrier, unable to see how the other is reacting or, more importantly, how they're feeling. Texting is great for logistics but bad for bonding with friends.

Face-to-face communication is natural, but inconvenient

Humans are highly attuned to human faces, making it the most natural and intimate way to communicate. However, it's also one of the most inconveinent. Although (video) calling is an option, it becomes increasingly more difficult to schedule for each additional group member.

Is there room for a new communication method that's both highly intimate, yet convenient too?


Finally, intimacy meets convenience

FaceChat brings the intimacy of a video call to the convinence of texting. Your reaction is automatically attached to the senders message, so your chat feels like a real live call. Anyone can contribute when they feel like, without missing the authenticity that comes from seeing live reactions and facial expressions.

Be together, even when you're not

FaceChat lets you feel present with your friends, even when they're not there. Anyone can express themselves, anytime, through automatically attached reactions, or even selfies.

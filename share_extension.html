iOS Share Extension

Kik Messenger

Since I was part of the content team during my internship at Kik, I worked on ways to share content. To make it easier for users to share photos and videos from their device I led the design of the first iOS share extension.

The Goals

The goals of this project were to make sharing content with people and groups on Kik as simple as possible. In addition, Kik was releasing new bubble styles with cool content previews at the time so it also needed to work well around all types of content.

Iterations

I explored a couple different iterations. There were three main ones:


1

2

3

1

The first iteration looked very much like a typical iOS extension. However, this solution was not optimal for our needs. It seemed like people preferred sharing with people in their recents list more that an alphabetical list of users. In addition, the amount of taps needed to share could still be reduced.


2

The second iteration focused on recent contacts and profile photos but this design had trouble displaying more than one name per chat, and this made group chats hard to distinguish since they could only display a single name. This design also bet on users always having profile photos, and it became difficult to choose the right recipients with only a first name.


3

The third iteration seemed to do the job the best. You see your recents and are proceeded by a page to write your message and confirm the content you're about to send. This version also worked great with our "preview bubbles."

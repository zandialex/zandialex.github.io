GIFs on Kik

Kik Messenger

After Kik acquired the GIF messaging app Relay we needed to add GIFs to the app. I designed the first GIF experience on the Kik app.

Problems and Constraints

  • There was no way for users to share GIFs

  • There should be a search feature

  • There should be a way to curate GIFs into collections

The Grid

Since we would need a way to display search results and collections, I explored different layouts to present multiple GIFs.

Masonry Style (2-Column)

The 3-column layout seemed quite small, especially since most users were using devices that were equal to or lesser than the size of an iPhone 5. After testing out the 2 column layout it looked like a better option.

I quickly realized that hardly any GIF has the same aspect ratio. Masonry style seemed like the best option since it displayed almost all GIFs at their true aspect ratio while still feeling organized.

Sponsored collections would ideally be curated by sponsors with GIFs about their product. A collection of GIFs about a TV season finale episode.

The icon

Since Kik had a UI pattern of displaying the content type on each bubble with an icon (this pattern was later removed), I created a new icon for GIFs.

Since there was no physical representation of a GIF (unlike an icon for the camera) I knew it would have to be a bit more symbolic than the other icons on the app. The icon should revolve around the ideas of expression, emotion, and fun.

After several iterations I liked the rainbow feeling. It felt very expressive. Had the colors been grouped in “warm”, “cool” or another small group of colors the associations could have communicated a specific emotion (cold/sadness or sunny/happy) but GIFs are used to express all sorts of emotions. The pixelated colors also helped communicate the feeling of “rawness” since GIFs are usually “unpolished.”
